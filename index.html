PLA (Solid Color-Matt)
PLA is a biodegradable and bio-based thermoplastic made primarily from renewable resources like corn starch, tapioca roots, or sugarcane. It is one of the most popular materials used in 3D printing due to its ease of use and the environmental benefits it offers over petroleum-based plastics. PLA is known for its good printability, making it an excellent choice for beginners and professionals.

TPU (Flexible Filament)
 TPU is known for its flexibility, which allows it to bend and stretch under strain without losing its original shape once the stress is removed. This makes it ideal for creating parts that require significant flexing, such as phone cases, flexible hinges, or wearable items.

PETG
PETG stands out from PLA in several key areas: it's stronger and more durable, making it suitable for functional parts that need to withstand stress or impact. PETG's higher temperature resistance means it won't deform easily in heat, and its slight flexibility prevents cracks under pressure. It also offers better resistance to chemicals and moisture, making it ideal for outdoor use or in humid environments. While PLA is praised for its biodegradability, PETG is recyclable, contributing to environmental sustainability. Additionally, PETG can achieve a clear or translucent finish for projects requiring a glass-like appearance.
